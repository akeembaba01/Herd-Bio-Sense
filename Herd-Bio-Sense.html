How to Interpret this Map

The map below shows your cow's health profile in relation to typical profiles of three different breeds. Use this to understand your cow's heat tolerance and overall health.

Health Zones:

Ideal Zone: Your cow is likely well-adapted and healthy.
Stressed Zone: Your cow may be struggling with heat or other issues.
Watchful Zone: Monitor this cow closely for changes in health.

Breeds on the Map:

Brangus: A balanced and adaptable breed.
Kedah-Kelantan: Naturally healthy and well-adapted to heat.
Holstein Friesian: Holstein Friesian: Often prone to heat stress and less adapted.

A Farmer's Guide to Using the Model

Match your cow's visible traits to the descriptions below:

Overall Health:

  • 1 (Low):Cow is lethargic, has a dull coat, or is visibly unwell.
  • 2 (Medium):Normal appearance and behavior for the breed.
  • 3 (High):Cow is very active, has a shiny coat, and is eating very well.

Hormonal Balance:

  • 1 (Low):Cow seems calm, has stable behavior, and a good appetite.
  • 2 (Medium):Average behavior.
  • 3 (High):Cow shows erratic or jittery behavior and a reduced appetite.

Heat Stress Level:

  • 1 (Low):Cow is in a cool, shaded environment with minimal physical stressors.
  • 2 (Medium):In average conditions, with some exposure to sun or heat.
  • 3 (High):Cow is in direct sunlight on a very hot day and shows signs of discomfort.
